Coding Resources

Here are some resources that I've found useful in my journey from librarian to web developer. If you have any feedback or suggestions for useful resources, let me know!

Getting Started

CodeNewbie:

Podcast and blog just for people learning to code. Interviews with people who work in a variety of development-related jobs. Learn about different types of work and resources for learners and job-seekers.

W3 Schools:

Tutorials on the basics (and more advanced features!) of HTML, JavaScript, CSS, and more. Includes "try it yourself" demos and explanations of individual elements and concepts.

JavaScript for Cats:

Learn the basics of JavaScript in a few hours. With cats!

Checkers/Validators from W3C:

Run your code through one of these validators to pick out errors. Also contains links to resources for checking your site for broken links and more.

Flexbox Froggy:

Move frogs around the page; learn how to use CSS to properly space and place content while you're at it.

Git Guide by Roger Dudler:

This super simple tutorial shows you how to use Git without a single wasted word.

Data Structures, Algorithms, & General Mathiness

RegexOne:

An interactive tutorial with lessons and practice problems to help you figure out how the heck to make regular expressions (a.k.a. regex).

Visualgo:

Funky animations help you visualize data structures and algorithms as you work through the written content.

BaseCS:

Hand-drawn illustrations keep it visually interesting so you can learn about hash tables AND stay awake. Each post comes with a list of resources and podcast.

Project Euler:

More than 600 problems to help up your math game. Primes, squares, Fibonacci, and tons more. Free account required.

Leveling Up

Upcase:

Up your game on topics ranging from shell scripting, Git, TDD, Ruby on Rails, regex, and many more. Topics are broken into trails for easy self-paced learning.

Codingame:

Gamify your way into learning or improving your skills in one of more than 20 languages. Python, Bash, JavaScript, Ruby, and loads more.

Design Academy:

A 5-part email course on design principles like color, typography, and more. (She also offers an in-depth course that is not free.) Check out her articles and recommended resources here.

Going Pro

LinkedIn:

Update your LinkedIn. Add every skill relevant to your knowledge base. Read the jobs page daily to find what skills employers want. Connect with recruiters, people you meet at events, and local tech community leaders. Check your feed for events and resources posted by your connections.

IRL Meetups:

I've found the coding community to be welcoming and supportive. Find meetups in your area and start connecting! Search Facebook, MeetUp, EventBrite, or the events calendars of local recruiters, coworking spaces, or libraries to see what is available in your area.

Jobscan:

Upload your resume and a job posting. Jobscan will tell you how likely your resume is to get past the ATS (automated tracking system, i.e. a resume-reviewing gatekeeper bot that decides whether your application gets passed on to a human for review or not.)

Codecademy's Python Tech Interview Practice:

Improve your whiteboarding skills and practice answering common technical interview questions.

Interviewing.io:

LIVE tech interview practice with engineers from companies like Google, Facebook, Microsoft, and more. Get feedback from an actual human, or watch past interviews and check out their blog to get tips.
